Frequently Asked Questions

Do eye massagers actually reduce puffiness and dark circles?

Eye massagers are effective for puffiness caused by fluid retention — air pressure and heat stimulate circulation and lymphatic drainage. Dark circles from vascular causes (bluish tones from visible blood vessels) respond well to improved circulation. Pigmentation-based dark circles need different treatments. For morning puffiness specifically, consistent daily use shows measurable improvement in most users within 2–3 weeks.

How long should you use an eye massager each session?

10–15 minutes is the designed session length for most devices — long enough for a full air-pressure cycle and meaningful heat penetration. Morning use targets overnight fluid accumulation; evening use relieves screen-related eye strain. Consistent daily use beats longer occasional sessions for both results.

Is it safe to use an eye massager every day?

Yes — daily use is safe for most people and produces the best results. If you have glaucoma, recent eye surgery, retinal detachment, or active eye inflammation, consult your ophthalmologist first. Always remove contact lenses before use. Start on the lowest pressure setting and work up to what feels comfortable.
