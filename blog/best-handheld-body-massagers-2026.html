Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a handheld massager and a massage gun?

Massage guns use rapid piston percussion (1,200–3,200 strokes/min) for deep post-workout muscle impact. Handheld massagers use lower-frequency vibration/oscillation for larger surface areas, general tension relief, and use on sensitive or bony areas where percussion would be uncomfortable. Both have a place — massage guns for specific post-workout recovery, handheld massagers for daily tension relief and circulation.

Is a corded or cordless massager better?

Corded massagers deliver more consistent motor power — they never lose intensity mid-session and typically provide 20–30% more vibration amplitude than battery equivalents. Cordless offers position freedom and travel convenience. For home use in fixed locations, corded delivers better power per dollar. For travel or using in multiple positions (lying down, in bed), cordless is worth the premium.

Where should you avoid using a handheld massager?

Avoid: directly on the spine or vertebrae, over open wounds, over blood clots, directly on the front of the neck (carotid sinus), over pacemakers or implanted devices, and on inflamed or swollen joints during acute flare-ups. Always apply to muscle bellies rather than directly over joints or bony structures.
