Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Dyson Airwrap actually worth $599?

For frequent stylists with fine or color-treated hair, yes. The Airwrap's Coanda effect never exceeds 302°F, which is dramatically lower than traditional curling irons. Over time, the reduced heat damage is real and visible. If you're a casual styler, the Shark FlexStyle at $249 or the Revlon One-Step at $47 will handle your needs without the premium.

What's the difference between a hot air brush and a blow dry styler?

A hot air brush (like the Revlon One-Step) dries and smooths simultaneously — it's one tool, one job, and it does it fast. A blow dry styler (Dyson Airwrap, Shark FlexStyle) uses interchangeable attachments to curl, wave, volumize, and smooth. Hot air brushes are faster and simpler; stylers are more versatile but take more time and skill to master.

Can I use a hot air brush on fine or color-treated hair?

Yes, but choose the right tool. The Dyson Airwrap is the safest — its temperature cap protects fragile hair. The Shark FlexStyle also uses controlled heat. For the Revlon One-Step, stick to medium heat and always apply a heat protectant. The BaByliss Pro runs very hot and is best suited for normal-to-thick hair types on medium settings.
