Frequently Asked Questions

Do face rollers actually work?

Yes — with realistic expectations. The rolling motion promotes lymphatic drainage, which temporarily reduces puffiness, especially around the jaw and eyes. When used after serums, rolling works products deeper into skin and measurably improves absorption. Chilling your roller for 10 minutes before use amplifies the de-puffing effect via vasoconstriction. What rollers won't do: lift sagging skin or replace professional treatments. The benefits are real, they're just modest — consistent daily use over weeks is where the results show up.

What's the difference between jade and rose quartz rollers?

Functionally, very little. Both are smooth and cool to the touch. Rose quartz stays cold longer when refrigerated, giving it a slight edge for de-puffing. Jade is slightly heavier, which some people prefer for a more massage-like feel. Neither stone has proven advantages over the other in clinical settings. The most important factor is whether the stone is genuine — cheap sets often use synthetic resin that's significantly less effective at staying cool and can break more easily.

How do I use a gua sha tool correctly?

Apply a facial oil or serum first so the tool glides without dragging skin. Hold the board at a 15–30° angle — almost flat against the face. Use light-to-medium pressure in long, sweeping upward strokes: up the neck, along the jawline, across cheekbones toward the temples. Repeat each stroke 3–5 times per zone. Always move toward lymph nodes (neck, ears, temples) to encourage drainage. Start 2–3 times per week; daily use is fine once your skin adapts.
