Frequently Asked Questions

How many shades can at-home kits realistically achieve?

Most OTC kits achieve 2–4 shades of improvement with consistent use. LED-accelerated 35% carbamide peroxide kits like AuraGlow push toward 4–6 shades. Professional in-office whitening achieves 6–10 shades but costs $300–800. Results depend heavily on stain type — extrinsic stains from coffee and wine respond well; intrinsic discoloration from antibiotics or trauma responds poorly to any OTC product.

Does hydrogen peroxide damage tooth enamel?

At OTC concentrations (3–10% HP or equivalent carbamide peroxide), research shows minimal to no enamel damage when used as directed. The primary concern is sensitivity and gum irritation. Enamel damage risk increases significantly from overuse beyond recommended cycles. PAP+ formulas like Hismile avoid this entirely, though with slightly less whitening power.

How long do whitening results last?

With normal coffee/tea consumption, results from a full treatment cycle last 3–6 months. Heavy staining beverages accelerate re-staining. Most users do 1–2 maintenance nights per month to preserve results. Naturally thin enamel (where yellow dentin shows through) makes dramatic whitening harder to maintain.
