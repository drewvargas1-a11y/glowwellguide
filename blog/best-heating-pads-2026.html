Frequently Asked Questions

What heating pad temperature is safe to use?

Most pads max at 140–170°F. For skin contact, below 115°F is safe for extended use. A medium setting (104–113°F) is typically most effective — hot enough to increase blood flow and relax muscles without burn risk. Never sleep with a heating pad under you, as compression concentrates heat. The 2-hour auto-shutoff on most quality pads is an important safety feature.

Moist heat vs. dry heat — which is better?

Moist heat penetrates deeper and is recommended for muscle knots, chronic tension, and menstrual cramps. Most quality pads include a moist heat option via a damp cover. Dry heat is easier to use and works well for general warming and surface muscle tension. Clinical studies generally favor moist heat for therapeutic use.

Can heating pads help with period cramps?

Yes — a 2001 study in Evidence-Based Nursing found continuous low-level heat was as effective as ibuprofen for primary dysmenorrhea. Heat relaxes uterine muscle and increases local blood flow. A large pad (12×12 inches minimum) at medium heat for 20–30 minute sessions provides the best relief. Pads with moist heat tend to be more effective than dry heat for cramps.
