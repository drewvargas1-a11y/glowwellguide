Frequently Asked Questions

How long should you lie on an acupressure mat?

Beginners: start with 5–10 minutes and build up over 1–2 weeks. Experienced users: 20–30 minutes is the standard session length. The first few minutes on the mat are uncomfortable as your body adjusts — most users describe a shift to warmth and deep relaxation around the 5-minute mark. Daily use produces the best cumulative results for back pain and stress. The mat can be used in the morning to energize or at night to wind down before sleep.

Do acupressure mats actually work for back pain?

There is moderate clinical evidence supporting acupressure mat use for low back pain. A 2012 study found statistically significant reductions in back pain and disability after 3 weeks of daily mat use. The mechanism is likely a combination of endorphin release triggered by spike stimulation, increased blood flow to back muscles, and the relaxation response reducing protective muscle guarding. Most consistent results are for chronic, non-specific low back pain rather than acute or structural pain.

Can you use an acupressure mat on bare skin?

Yes, and bare skin typically produces stronger effects since the spikes make direct contact with skin nerve endings. Start with a thin layer of clothing if you're new to the mat — the spikes on bare skin feel significantly more intense initially. After 1–2 weeks of regular use, most people adapt and prefer bare skin for the deeper, faster relaxation response it produces. Avoid the mat if you have open skin lesions, sunburn, or very sensitive skin conditions.
