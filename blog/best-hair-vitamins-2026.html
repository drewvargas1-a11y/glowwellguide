— Ranked by Evidence & Value

Hair supplements range from simple gummy vitamins to multi-pathway clinical formulas recommended by dermatologists. The affordable gummy options (SugarBearHair, Hims Biotin) provide general hair-supportive nutrition but have less targeted efficacy. We ranked four top-selling options with honest assessments of what each actually does — and for whom.

Hims positions its hair gummies for men, and the formula reflects that: beyond the standard biotin and multivitamin base, Hims includes saw palmetto extract — a DHT (dihydrotestosterone) inhibitor. Frequently Asked Questions

Does biotin actually help hair growth?

Only if you're deficient — which is rare. For people with adequate biotin levels, extra biotin is just excreted. The biotin-for-hair marketing is overblown for most people. Supplements addressing multiple pathways (Nutrafol, Viviscal) have stronger evidence than biotin-only formulas. Iron and Vitamin D deficiencies are actually more commonly linked to hair loss — ask your doctor for a blood panel before supplementing.

What vitamins are actually important for hair health?

Iron (ferritin — deficiency causes telogen effluvium), Vitamin D (linked to alopecia areata), Zinc, Vitamin B12, and Omega-3s have the strongest hair-related evidence. Get a blood panel (ferritin, Vitamin D, B12, thyroid) before spending on supplements — deficiency testing is more cost-effective than supplementing everything.

How long do hair vitamins take to show results?

Take supplements for at least 3 months before evaluating (one full hair growth cycle). The most reliable indicator: reduced shedding in the shower. Visible thickness improvement takes 4–6 months. If no change at 6 months, the supplement isn't addressing your specific root cause.
