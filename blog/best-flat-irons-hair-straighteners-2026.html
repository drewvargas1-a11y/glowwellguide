Dyson Corrale

★★★★½ 4.5 / 5.0 Best Cordless $450

The Dyson Corrale's genuinely unique feature is its flexing manganese copper alloy plates — they physically bend to conform to the curvature of each hair strand as you glide through. Conventional flat plates can only contact hair at its widest point, leaving edges with uneven pressure and heat. Dyson's flexing plates, by wrapping around strands, allow you to achieve the same straightening result at a temperature 130°F lower (per Dyson's published research) — which is the theoretical heat damage reduction argument. In our testing, the Corrale did require fewer passes than the BaBylissPRO on curly hair at equivalent temperatures. The cordless operation (up to 30 minutes per charge) is genuinely useful for travel. The $450 price is the sticking point — for home use, the GHD Platinum+ delivers comparable or better results at nearly half the cost. But if you travel frequently and want the most damage-reducing technology available, the Corrale is the pick.

Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature should a flat iron be set at?

Fine or damaged hair: 300–325°F. Normal/medium hair: 340–375°F. Thick or coarse hair: 375–410°F. Use the lowest temperature that achieves your desired result in a single pass — multiple passes at lower temp cause more total heat exposure than one efficient pass at higher temp. Always use a heat protectant.

Ceramic vs. titanium — which plate is better?

Ceramic heats more evenly and gently — better for fine, damaged, or color-treated hair. Titanium heats faster and holds higher temperatures under thick sections — better for coarse, thick, or resistant hair. The GHD Platinum+ uses a proprietary ceramic-titanium hybrid that combines both. When unsure, ceramic is the safer default for most hair types.

How often can you safely flat iron your hair?

2–3 times per week is generally safe with proper heat protectant. Daily straightening will cause measurable structural damage over months even with protectant. Key practices: always use heat protectant, iron only fully dry hair, work in sections rather than multiple passes over the same strands, and deep condition weekly if straightening regularly.
