Frequently Asked Questions

What size curling wand is best for my hair length?

Short hair (chin to shoulder): ¾" to 1" barrel for defined ringlets or tight waves. Medium hair (shoulder to bra strap): 1" to 1.25" is the most versatile, producing tight curls or loose waves depending on hold time and wrap angle. Long hair: 1.25" to 1.5" barrel for the loose, flowing waves most associated with long-hair styles. Conical (tapered) wands create a natural-looking mix of curl sizes across the same section of hair.

How do you make curls last longer?

Start with completely dry hair — even slight moisture causes curls to drop faster. Apply a mousse or curl-enhancing product before styling. Work in small sections (1" maximum) for tighter, longer-lasting curls. After releasing each curl, hold it in your palm as it cools completely before letting go — this sets the shape. Finish with a light-hold hairspray. Sleeping on a silk pillowcase prevents the friction that breaks down curls overnight.

Wand vs. clamp curling iron — which is better?

Wands (no clamp) produce softer, more natural-looking beach waves — the wrapping technique creates organic variation. Clamp irons produce more uniform, polished ringlets with a consistent ring shape. Wands are better for effortless everyday styles; clamps are better for defined, formal curls. Most professional stylists prefer wands for daily use and reach for clamp irons when a client requests a structured, high-polish look.
