— Ranked by Effectiveness

Hair loss treatments fall into three distinct categories: topicals (minoxidil), devices (LLLT laser therapy), and supplements (nutraceuticals). They work by different mechanisms and aren't mutually exclusive — many dermatologists recommend combining approaches. Below we rank the best options in each category: FDA-cleared minoxidil, clinically validated laser therapy, and evidence-backed supplements, with honest assessments of how long they take, what type of hair loss they address, and what to expect.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does minoxidil take to work?

Initial results at 3–4 months, significant improvement at 6 months. Expect a brief shedding phase at weeks 2–6 (normal — the treatment is cycling out old hairs). Results continue improving through 12 months. Stopping minoxidil causes hair loss to return within 3–6 months — continued use is required indefinitely.

What is LLLT and does laser therapy actually work?

LLLT (Low-Level Laser Therapy) uses 650nm red light to stimulate follicles via photobiomodulation. Multiple RCTs show 20–35% improvement in hair density at 6 months — similar to minoxidil. FDA-cleared devices (like iRestore) are cleared for safety, not efficacy. Works best in early-stage hair loss; can't restore completely lost follicles.

Is Nutrafol worth the price at $88/month?

For stress-related, postpartum, or hormonal hair loss — yes. Nutrafol's multi-pathway approach addresses root causes minoxidil doesn't. For genetic male or female pattern baldness (androgenetic alopecia), minoxidil is more cost-effective with stronger independent evidence. The company-funded trial shows 80% improvement at 6 months, but independent validation is limited at this price point.
