Frequently Asked Questions

What wattage do I need for a gel nail lamp?

36W is the minimum for reliable curing, but 48W is the sweet spot for home use — it cures most gel polishes in 30–60 seconds and handles gel-builder products that lower-wattage lamps struggle with. The key metric alongside wattage is wavelength: dual UV/LED lamps (365nm + 405nm) cure the broadest range of gel formulas.

Can gel nail lamps damage your skin?

UV exposure during gel curing is real but very low for typical at-home use (30–60 seconds per coat). For frequent users, applying SPF 30+ sunscreen to the backs of hands before curing is a reasonable precaution — the cumulative exposure over months of weekly sessions is the primary concern, not any single session.

How long do DIY gel nails last vs. salon gel?

With proper prep and technique, DIY gel nails can last 14–21 days — comparable to salon results. Key factors: proper nail dehydration before application, applying thin coats, and full-contact curing with fingers spread flat. Most at-home lifting failures are prep or technique issues, not lamp quality.
