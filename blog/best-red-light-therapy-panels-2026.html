Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to see results from red light therapy?

For skin texture and collagen improvement, expect 4–8 weeks of consistent daily use. Muscle recovery benefits are often felt within the first few sessions. The key variable is irradiance — panels delivering 50–150 mW/cm² at the recommended treatment distance produce results far faster than underpowered devices. A 10–20 minute session at 6 inches is the standard protocol for most clinical results.

What wavelengths should a red light panel have?

The two most clinically validated wavelengths are 660nm (red) and 850nm (near-infrared). 660nm penetrates 1–2mm for skin rejuvenation and collagen production. 850nm penetrates 30–40mm for muscle recovery, joint health, and deep inflammation. A panel with both covers all primary use cases. Some premium panels add 630nm and 810nm for broader coverage — the Rouge Pro includes 5 wavelengths for under $200.

How far should you stand from the panel?

6–12 inches is optimal for most panels — this is where irradiance is highest and treatment time shortest (10–20 minutes). Standing farther reduces irradiance exponentially. Each panel's manual should list the irradiance at various distances — this number (mW/cm²) tells you the actual light dose you're receiving and lets you calculate the correct treatment time for your goals.
