Frequently Asked Questions

How long should you use an infrared sauna blanket per session?

30–45 minutes, 3–4 times per week is the standard recommendation. Start at 20 minutes and a lower temperature for your first few sessions. Drink 16–24 oz of water beforehand and hydrate immediately after. Don't use within 2 hours of a large meal, and avoid if pregnant or you have cardiovascular conditions without physician approval.

Do infrared sauna blankets actually detox your body?

The detox claim is nuanced — sweat does contain small amounts of heavy metals and other compounds, but the liver and kidneys are your primary detox organs. What research more clearly supports is improved circulation, reduced muscle soreness, lower cortisol, and improved sleep quality. A 2018 JAMA Internal Medicine study found regular sauna use associated with reduced cardiovascular mortality. The recovery and relaxation benefits are well-documented.

Are infrared sauna blankets safe?

Yes, for most healthy adults. Stay hydrated, don't exceed 45-minute sessions, never use while sleeping, and choose a low-EMF certified blanket. Avoid if you have a pacemaker, implanted medical device, uncontrolled hypertension, or a condition that impairs sweating. When in doubt, consult your physician first.
