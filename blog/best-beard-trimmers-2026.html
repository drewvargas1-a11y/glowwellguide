Frequently Asked Questions

What length setting is "stubble"?

1–3mm = heavy 5 o'clock shadow stubble. 3–5mm = full designer stubble. 5–10mm = short beard start. The classic "5 o'clock shadow" look is usually achieved at 1.5–2mm. If your trimmer's minimum guard is 3mm, use the bare blade carefully in even passes at a consistent 45° angle to get shorter results without irritation.

How do you prevent razor bumps with a beard trimmer?

Always trim in the direction of hair growth — never against for trimming (going against is for shaving only). Clean the blade before and after every use. Apply a lightweight pre-shave oil before trimming to reduce friction. If you have coarse or curly hair, the Bevel T1's blade design specifically reduces the bump-causing drag that conventional trimmer heads create on tightly coiled hair patterns. Post-trim, apply a light alcohol-free toner to close the pores.

How often should you replace beard trimmer blades?

Self-sharpening blades like Wahl's technically never need replacement. Standard stainless blades need sharpening or replacement roughly every 6–12 months depending on use frequency and beard density. The clearest signs: snagging or tugging instead of clean cutting, increased noise during operation, or uneven cutting requiring multiple passes on the same area. Some trimmers sell replacement blade heads; others require buying a new unit when blades dull.
