Frequently Asked Questions

Are electric toothbrushes actually better than manual?

Yes, consistently. A 2019 Cochrane review of 56 studies found oscillating-rotating electric toothbrushes reduced plaque by 21% more and gingivitis by 11% more than manual brushing. The built-in 2-minute timer alone is a significant real-world advantage — most people brush for under 45 seconds with a manual brush. Even the $50 Oral-B Pro 1000 outperforms manual brushing in clinical settings.

What's the difference between oscillating and sonic toothbrushes?

Oscillating-rotating brushes (Oral-B) have a small round head that rotates and pulses — they have the most clinical evidence for plaque removal. Sonic brushes (Philips Sonicare, Burst) use a back-and-forth motion at very high speeds and feel gentler on gums. If you have healthy gums and want maximum plaque removal, go Oral-B. If you have sensitivity or gum disease, Sonicare's gentler motion is a better fit.

How often should I replace my electric toothbrush head?

Every 3 months — same as a manual toothbrush. Most premium models include bristle-fade indicators that change color when replacement is due. Worn bristles lose their cleaning angle and are significantly less effective. Compatible aftermarket heads on Amazon cost $6–$8 for packs and work just as well as brand-name replacements.
