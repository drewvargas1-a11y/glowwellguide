Frequently Asked Questions

Does dry brushing actually reduce cellulite?

Dry brushing temporarily improves cellulite appearance by boosting circulation and lymphatic drainage — reducing fluid retention for a smoother look for a few hours. There's no clinical evidence it permanently eliminates cellulite. The proven benefits are mechanical exfoliation, improved circulation, and the self-care ritual benefit. Real long-term improvements to cellulite appearance come from hydration, body composition, and collagen-supporting skincare.

How should I dry brush for best results?

Brush on dry skin before showering. Start at feet, sweep upward toward the heart in long firm strokes — following the direction of lymphatic drainage. Use circular motions on stomach and chest. Duration: 3–5 minutes. Pressure: firm but not painful — mild redness (circulation) is normal, irritation is not. Shower after to remove dead skin cells, then apply body oil while skin is slightly damp.

How often should you dry brush?

3–4 times per week is ideal. Daily is fine for tolerant skin; avoid daily use on sensitive or thin skin areas. Start with 2x per week and increase as your skin adapts. Signs of over-brushing: persistent redness beyond 30 minutes, raw patches, or increased sensitivity. Replace the brush every 6–12 months as bristles soften and lose effectiveness.
