Foil vs Rotary Shavers 2026
— Best Picks for Every Shaving Style

The foil vs rotary debate has a real answer — it just depends on your beard and your face. Foil shavers use oscillating blades behind a thin metal screen to cut hair precisely at skin level, making them ideal for straight beards, flat jaw lines, and daily shavers who want the closest possible finish. Rotary shavers use three or four spinning heads that flex and pivot to follow facial contours, making them the better choice for curved jaw structures, longer stubble, and anyone who shaves every 2–3 days. We tested the best of each type — from a $79 budget foil to the $299 flagship rotary — so you can buy with confidence.

Frequently Asked Questions

Foil vs rotary shaver — which gives a closer shave?

Foil shavers consistently deliver a closer shave on flat areas like the cheeks, upper lip, and chin. The oscillating blades cut very precisely at skin level. Rotary shavers close the gap on curved areas like the jaw and neck, but on flat areas foil wins. If closeness is your priority and you shave daily, choose a foil shaver like the Panasonic Arc5 or Braun Series 9 Pro+.

Which type of shaver is better for sensitive skin?

Rotary shavers usually cause less irritation because the flexible heads adjust to facial contours rather than pressing a rigid foil screen against the skin. The Philips Norelco 9900's heads flex in 10 directions to minimize friction. That said, if your irritation comes from multiple passes, the Panasonic Arc5's 70,000 cuts/min speed cuts clean in one pass and may actually be gentler overall.

Can I use an electric shaver every day without skin damage?

Yes — premium electric shavers are designed for daily use and are far gentler than manual razors over time. Use light pressure, let the shaver do the work, and keep the heads clean. Dirty foils are the leading cause of electric shaver irritation — not the technology itself. Clean after every use and replace foils every 12–18 months.
