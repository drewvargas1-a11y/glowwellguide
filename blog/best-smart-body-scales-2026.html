Best Smart Body Scales (2026)
— Track More Than Weight

Smart body composition scales have transformed the bathroom scale from a single-number verdict into a comprehensive wellness dashboard. By sending a safe, imperceptible electrical current through the body (bioelectrical impedance analysis, or BIA), they estimate body fat percentage, muscle mass, bone density, and visceral fat — metrics that tell a far more complete story of health progress than weight alone. Someone losing fat while gaining muscle may see no change on a traditional scale, but a smart scale shows the body recomposition happening. They sync automatically to health apps (Apple Health, Google Fit, Garmin Connect, Withings Health Mate), making tracking effortless. We tested four of the best-reviewed models at every budget level to identify the right smart scale for your goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are smart scales for body fat percentage?

Smart scales use bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA) — reasonably accurate for tracking trends but can vary 3–5% in absolute accuracy vs. DEXA scans. Accuracy improves when you weigh at the same time each day in the same hydration state. Morning measurements (post-bathroom, pre-eating) are most consistent. Use the scale to track your personal trend, not to compare to clinical standards.

What's the best time of day to weigh yourself?

First thing in the morning — after using the bathroom, before eating or drinking. Your weight can fluctuate 3–5 lbs throughout the day based on food, water, and sodium. Weighing at the same time removes this variable and shows true trends. Weekly averages rather than single daily readings give the clearest picture of actual progress.

What metrics do smart body composition scales measure?

Beyond weight, most smart scales measure body fat %, muscle mass, bone mass, body water %, visceral fat index, BMI, and metabolic age. The Eufy P2 Pro measures 16 metrics. Accuracy hierarchy: weight is most reliable, followed by body fat %, then muscle mass. Visceral fat and metabolic age are directional estimates. Focus on weight, body fat %, and muscle mass for the most reliable tracking.
