Best Sleep Masks (2026)
— Tested & Ranked

Even small amounts of light during sleep suppress melatonin and fragment REM cycles. A quality sleep mask is one of the highest-ROI sleep upgrades you can make — and they cost $10–$35. The key differentiator between a great mask and a cheap one isn't just the material: it's whether it maintains a true blackout under pressure (when you roll onto your side), whether it stays put without constricting your head, and whether the eye area design actually works for your face shape. We tested four of the top-rated options across these criteria.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do sleep masks actually improve sleep quality?

Yes — light exposure suppresses melatonin and can fragment sleep cycles even at low levels. Research from the University of Flinders found that sleep mask users fell asleep faster and had improved REM sleep and next-day alertness. The effect is strongest for shift workers, light sleepers, and anyone sleeping during daylight hours.

Is silk actually better than polyester for sleep masks?

For most people, yes. Genuine mulberry silk is temperature-regulating, hypoallergenic, and doesn't absorb skincare products — active ingredients stay on your skin instead of transferring to the fabric. The friction reduction is also gentler on the delicate eye area. Polyester can perform well for pure light-blocking without the skincare benefits.

What's the best sleep mask for side sleepers?

Side sleepers need a mask that doesn't get displaced by pillow pressure or press against the eyes when the face is sideways. The Manta is designed specifically for this — its independently adjustable cups maintain a zero-pressure pocket even under pillow contact. The HOLSSI 3D mask works well too at a lower price. Flat silk masks typically press against eyelids for side sleepers.
