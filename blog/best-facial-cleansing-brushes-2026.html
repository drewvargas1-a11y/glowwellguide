Frequently Asked Questions

Are facial cleansing brushes actually better than washing with your hands?

Yes — significantly so. Studies on sonic cleansing devices show they remove 6× more makeup, twice as much dirt, and significantly more oil than hand-washing. The mechanical action breaks up pore-clogging debris that fingers push around. Over time, consistent use improves skin texture, reduces congestion, and enhances serum absorption. The key: use the right brush for your skin type — silicone (Foreo) for sensitive, bristle for oily/congested.

How often should you use a facial cleansing brush?

3–5 times per week is ideal for most skin types. Sensitive or rosacea-prone skin should start once per week and increase slowly. Normal-to-oily skin can handle daily use, especially with a gentle silicone brush like the Foreo Luna 4. Always use it at night — morning cleansing doesn't need this level of power. Follow with toner, serum, and moisturizer while skin is still damp.

What's the difference between silicone and bristle cleansing brushes?

Silicone brushes (Foreo, PMD Clean) are non-porous — they don't harbor bacteria, dry quickly, and never need head replacement. They're gentler and better for sensitive or dry skin. Bristle brushes (Michael Todd, Vanity Planet) provide more physical exfoliation — better for normal-to-oily skin. Bristle heads need replacement every 3 months and must fully dry between uses to avoid bacterial buildup.
