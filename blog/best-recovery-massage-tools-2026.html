Frequently Asked Questions

Do massage guns actually help with muscle recovery?

Yes — the science is solid. Percussive therapy increases local blood flow, reduces DOMS, and improves range of motion when used before and after exercise. Studies show 2–3 minutes per muscle group post-workout reduces next-day soreness by 30–40%. Premium guns like the Theragun Elite's 16mm amplitude penetrate deeper, but even the $65 Bob & Brad D6 Pro delivers measurable recovery benefits.

How long should I use a massage gun per session?

2–3 minutes per muscle group is the sweet spot for post-workout recovery. For pre-workout activation, 30 seconds per group on a fast speed is enough to stimulate blood flow. For chronic tension knots, up to 5 minutes per area — but don't exceed that. Always move the device slowly (about 1 inch per second) rather than holding it in one spot to avoid bruising.

Is a Theragun worth the price over a budget massage gun?

For regular athletes or chronic muscle tension sufferers, yes. The Theragun Elite's 16mm amplitude penetrates far deeper than a budget gun's 8–10mm, and the ergonomic handle lets you reach your own back and shoulders. For casual users who want occasional soreness relief, the Bob & Brad D6 Pro at $65 or Hypervolt Go 2 at $179 deliver excellent value. Amplitude is the deciding factor.
